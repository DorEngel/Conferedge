The Story

I started in cybersecurity, building and breaking systems for startups. Then I became a professional mentalist. Weird combination, I know - but it turns out that understanding how technology works and how people think is a pretty useful combo.

These days I don't just perform; companies fly me to conferences around the world to deliver their messages in an original and entertaining way - I draw massive crowds, qualify leads mid-show, weave product messaging into mind-bending moments, and hand warm prospects directly to sales teams.

I run ConferEdge, where I help B2B tech companies stop treating conferences like an expensive lottery ticket and start seizing the opportunity of conferences as a massive pipeline channel. I've worked with cybersecurity startups, cloud infrastructure companies, logistics companies and everything in between. I've seen what works on the floor, what quietly bleeds budgets dry, and I'm happy to share all about it, no gatekeeping.

Speaking Topics

Things I Love Talking About

Topic 01

5 Free Shifts That Drastically Increase Your Conference ROI

I see this all the time: companies throw more money at events instead of rethinking their approach. Bigger booth, flashier swag, same mediocre results. The problem was never the budget.

Five zero-budget changes to booth strategy, staff behavior, and messaging that actually move the needle on qualified conversations and booked meetings.

Topic 02

Followup Is Dead: Why Your Post-Event Emails Are Burning Pipeline

Here's the uncomfortable truth: by the time you send that "great meeting you!" email, your prospect has 200 new business cards and zero memory of your booth. The window already closed.

A step-by-step playbook for creating pipeline during the event - so follow-up becomes a formality, not a Hail Mary.

Topic 03

I Left a Career in Tech to Become a Mentalist - Here's What I Learned

Most people have a thing they'd rather be doing. Very few actually make the jump. I walked away from a comfortable cybersecurity career to become a professional mentalist - and then built a business around it. It wasn't smooth, and it wasn't obvious.

An honest, no-fluff conversation about career pivots - what it actually takes to leave the safe path, how to test your next thing before you leap, and why the skills from your "old life" are more transferable than you think.

Topic 04

The Invisible Qualifier: Using Psychology to Separate Buyers from Browsers

Badge scans don't tell you who's ready to buy - they tell you who wanted free chocolate. Your sales team is wasting hours on people who were never going to convert, and missing the ones who were.

Mentalism-based techniques for reading body language, steering conversations, and spotting real buying signals - so every minute on the floor actually counts.
